\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Benchmarks}

In order to evaluate the performance in terms of both regret and computational efficiency, we consider different problem classes.

\textit{Knapsack (KP).} We generate 0-1 KP datsets following the procedure proposed 
by \cite{elmachtoub2022smart}. We will refer to this type of dataset as \textit{KP-50}. 
We assume that one between the weights, the values and the capacity has to be predicted, 
while the other two are known. In particular, we refer to the datset in which the weights are unknown as \textit{KP-50 weights}.

\subsection{Baselines}

Our baselines include a predictive model trained for maximum likelihood estimation, 
referred to as Prediction Focused Learning (PFL) model, 
and state-of-the-art models for DFL.
